1 — Executive summary
- Product: Cotopia Profit Calculator — web-based tool for pricing, profit margin, scenario analysis and export.
- USP: Simple UX, scenario planning, CSV export, Chart.js visualizations; target: entrepreneurs, digital product sellers, developers building tools, and investors evaluating margins.
- Business goal: monetize via licensing/subscriptions to small businesses, sell developer licenses and white-label opportunities, attract strategic investment for product development and go-to-market.

2 — Product positioning & value propositions
- For entrepreneurs / sellers: "Quickly calculate price, margins and scenario outcomes to make profitable pricing decisions."
  - Benefits: reduce pricing errors, faster decision-making, printable/CSV exports for accounting.
- For developers / agencies: "Embeddable, customizable calculator to integrate into SaaS dashboards or client projects."
  - Benefits: easy integration, customization, licensing for redistribution.
- For investors: "Simple, high-margin SaaS or license play with recurring revenue potential and clear expandability (analytics, integrations)."

3 — Target customers & buyer personas
- Solo entrepreneurs / creators: low budget, prefer self-serve sign-up, value templates and instant insights.
- Small & medium ecommerce sellers: need bulk/CSV import/export, integrations (Shopify, WooCommerce).
- Agencies / developers: want white-label, API or embeddable widget, commercial license for client projects.
- Finance teams & investors: need accuracy, audit logs, advanced reporting and multi-scenario forecasting.

4 — Licensing & pricing models (recommended)
Offer multiple tiers and license types so buyers can choose fit.

License types
- End-User SaaS Subscription (hosted by Cotopia)
  - Billed monthly/annual. Multi-seat options.
  - Pros: predictable recurring revenue, easier upgrades/analytics.
- Perpetual License (one-time) + annual maintenance
  - Provide source-available/minified code; restrict redistribution.
  - Pros: larger upfront payment; cons: maintenance overhead.
- Developer / Agency License
  - Redistribution / white-label license with revenue share or higher flat fee.
  - Include API/SDK, custom branding rights.
- OEM / Enterprise (custom)
  - On-prem or private SaaS instance with SLAs, custom integrations.
- Freemium + Paid Pro
  - Free tier with core features, paid unlock advanced features (scenarios, export formats, team seats).

Pricing tier example (replace with local market research)
- Free: basic single-product calculation, 1 scenario, CSV export disabled.
- Pro: $9–$29 / month — unlimited calculations, scenario comparisons, CSV export, basic email support.
- Team: $49–$199 / month — multi-user, team permissions, audit logs, SSO.
- Agency / Developer: $499–$2,500 one-time or $99–$499 / month — white-label + redistribution rights.
- Enterprise: custom — includes SLA, uptime guarantees, data retention & compliance.

Discounts & promotions
- Annual subscription discount (10–20%).
- Nonprofit / educational discount.
- Volume discounts for agencies.

Payment models & flows
- Use Stripe (recommended) for subscriptions, invoicing, and marketplace compliance.
- For international buyers, support multiple currencies or convert via Stripe.
- For high-value Enterprise sales, use Net-30 invoicing and require purchase orders (POs).

5 — Legal & IP considerations (Wyoming/US)
- IP ownership
  - Cotopia should retain copyrights on code and branding.
  - Register copyright for code and creative assets.
  - Consider trademark registration for “Cotopia” and product name.
- Licensing terms
  - Clear license grants (what is permitted), restrictions (no redistribution unless licensed), termination, and liability limits.
- Incorporation & contracts
  - Ensure Cotopia (Wyoming entity) is properly incorporated and has operating agreement.
  - Use standard Master Services Agreement (MSA) for custom work, and EULA / SaaS Terms for hosted product.
- Data processing & privacy
  - Publish a Privacy Policy (GDPR/CCPA-aware if serving EU/CA users).
  - If processing EU data, consider Data Processing Addendum (DPA).
- Taxes & nexus
  - Wyoming business friendly: know your sales tax obligations for digital goods in states you sell to. Consult tax advisor.
- Export controls
  - Verify whether encryption or certain features trigger export control rules (unlikely, but check).
- Liability & disclaimers
  - Disclaim financial or tax advice; recommend consulting professionals. Limit liability to subscription fees.

6 — Contracts & template terms (sales, TOS, SLA, privacy)
Create the following documents:
- Terms of Service / EULA (hosted and downloadable versions).
  - Key sections: license grant, permitted use, restrictions, fees, refunds, updates, termination, indemnity, warranties, limitation of liability, governing law (Wyoming), dispute resolution.
- Privacy Policy
  - Explain collected data, storage duration, third-party processors (Stripe, Google Analytics), cookie policy.
- Data Processing Addendum (DPA)
  - If serving EU/EEA customers and processing personal data.
- SLA (Enterprise)
  - Uptime target (e.g., 99.9%), incident response times, credits for downtime, maintenance windows.
- Developer / Reseller Agreement
  - Define redistribution rights, branding, revenue share, support obligations, confidentiality.
- Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)
  - For investor discussions and enterprise prospects.

Include contact: support@cotopia.org in each page for policy questions.

7 — Revenue, billing & payments
- Payment provider: Stripe (or equivalent). Use Stripe Billing for subscriptions.
- Invoicing: integrate with QuickBooks / Xero for accounting.
- Refund policy: define clear refund windows (e.g., 14 days for annual plans).
- Trials: 7–14 day free trial for Pro features; collect card or keep no-card trial (A/B test).
- Fraud prevention: enable Stripe Radar; set velocity rules.
- VAT handling: use Stripe VAT features for EU; collect VAT IDs for B2B.

11 — Technical architecture, deployment & operations
Hosting & infra
- SaaS: host on reliable cloud (AWS, GCP, or Azure). Use managed services (RDS, S3, CloudFront).
- Consider a multi-tenant architecture with tenant isolation, or single-tenant Enterprise deployments on request.
- Use containers (Docker) + Kubernetes or managed container services (ECS, GKE).

Components
- Frontend: static SPA with Chart.js; CDN-backed hosting (Vercel, Netlify, S3+CloudFront).
- Backend: Node.js / Python REST API for user, billing, analytics.
- Database: PostgreSQL for transactional data; Redis for caches/sessions.
- File storage: S3 for export files and backups.
- Logging & monitoring: Sentry, Datadog/Prometheus + Grafana, and CloudWatch.

CI/CD
- GitHub Actions or GitLab CI for builds and tests.
- Automated tests (unit, integration, e2e).
- Automated deployment pipelines with blue/green or canary releases.

Backup & DR
- Daily database backups retained per policy (30/90/365 days tiers).
- Regular DR drills and runbooks.
- Cross-region replicas for critical enterprise instances.

12 — Security, privacy & compliance
Security basics
- Secure coding practices, input validation, output encoding.
- Use HTTPS everywhere, HSTS, secure cookies.
- Authentication: OAuth2 / JWT for APIs; allow SSO (SAML / OIDC) for Enterprise.
- Password storage with bcrypt/argon2 and rate-limiting login attempts.

Encryption & keys
- Store secrets in a secrets manager (AWS Secrets Manager, HashiCorp Vault).
- Encrypt sensitive data at rest (database) and in transit (TLS 1.2+).

Vulnerability management
- Regular dependency scanning (Dependabot, Snyk).
- Periodic penetration testing and third-party security audits.

Privacy & compliance
- GDPR: data subject rights, retention policies, DPA for EU customers.
- CCPA: consumer rights for California residents.
- PCI: if storing/processing card data, use Stripe to avoid PCI scope.
- Privacy Policy: name data collected, processors, retention, contact (support@cotopia.org).

16 — Financial projections / unit economics (template)
Unit economics (example)
- Average revenue per user (ARPU): $X / month
- Gross margin: revenue minus direct costs (hosting, Stripe fees), target >70%.
- CAC: cost to acquire a paying customer (marketing + sales).
- LTV: ARPU * average customer lifetime (months) * gross margin.
- Payback period: CAC / (ARPU * gross margin)

Basic projection template (monthly)
- Users: free signups, paid conversions, churn
- Revenue: monthly subscriptions, one-time license sales, services
- Costs: hosting, development, marketing, support, G&A
- Cashflow & runway: burn rate, operating expenses, fundraising needs

17 — Risks & mitigations
Product risks
- Low product-market fit — mitigation: rapid user testing and interviews, build templates, iterate.
- Competition — mitigation: focus on developer experience and embeddability, partnerships.

Technical risks
- Scalability/performance — mitigation: autoscaling, caching, load testing.
- Security breaches — mitigation: audits, monitoring, incident response plan.

Business risks
- Payment/regulatory — mitigation: use compliant payment processors, consult tax advisors.
- Contributor/third-party license conflicts — mitigation: license audit, use permissive libs or secure permissions.
